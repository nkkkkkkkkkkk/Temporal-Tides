Temporal Tides {{ item.title }} Purpose of Temporal Tides
Temporal Tides is a card-matching game that explores the relationship between whales and global trade through the lens of time. This module contributes to the broader theme of the webpage, which examines how human economic systems intersect with whale ecosystems.

A central idea in this module is that the effects of human activity on whales are not evenly experienced throughout the year. As other modules on this webpage highlight impacts such as whale health effects, this module extends that discussion by showing how these impacts often become more pronounced during particular periods. Certain times of the year bring intensified shipping activity or seasonal trade flows. At the same time, whales move through key phases of their life cycles, including migration and breeding. When these human and ecological timelines overlap, the consequences for whales can become especially significant. The game draws on Rupert Griffiths’ concept of “more-than-human temporalities” in the Anthropocene. Human societies tend to organize time through standardized systems such as months, seasons, and economic cycles. Non-human organisms, however, follow ecological rhythms shaped by factors such as migration patterns and food availability.

To make these different temporal systems visible, Temporal Tides uses the a familiar framework: months of the year. Each pair of cards represents two case studies that occur during overlapping time periods and geographic locations but belong to different systems. One card reflects human economic activity, such as peak shipping seasons, while the other reflects whale biological activity, such as migration routes. Matching these pairs reveals how the same moment in time can hold very different meanings depending on whether it is viewed through a human economic lens or an ecological one.

This game is inspired by the familiar memory-matching format, but the pairs are complementary rather than identical. Instead of matching identical images, players match two related events that occur during the same time period. When a card is flipped, a dialog window appears that requires the player to pause and read the information before continuing. This design draws on Brock’s work in rhetorical code studies, which suggests that digital systems shape how users engage with information. In this case, the game’s interface encourages players to reflect on how human timelines intersect with ecological ones through the lens of temporality.

 How to Play Temporal Tides
• The game contains 8 cards (4 pairs) placed face down.
• Click on a card to flip it over and reveal the information on the card.
• Each card represents either human economic activity or whale biological activity.
• A pair connects two related events that occur during the same time period and location, but represent different temporal systems.
• Instead of matching identical cards, you are matching two complementary cases, one showing a human activity and the other showing whale activity.
• When a card is flipped, a dialog window will appear with more information. Read the information before continuing.
• Continue matching cards until all pairs have been found.
